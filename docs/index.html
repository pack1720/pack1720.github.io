Cub Scout Pack 1720

of Cedar Park, Texas

Open to Boy and Girls, Grades K-5

Join our Pack!

Cub Scouts Pack 1720, is located in Cedar Park, a suburb Northwest of Austin Texas. We are part of the North Shore District of the Capitol Area Council, and our Charter Organization is Peace Lutheran Church.

The Scouting Program provides a safe environment for kids to learn and grow through age-appropriate challenges. It's designed to be fun so that this growth experience isn’t like school or the military. Many of the activities can involve the whole family, whatever that looks like. Scouting is about raising the next generation of adults. It is the kind of enrichment that builds the whole person into the kind of adults that we all want them to become.

Our Scouts can participate in a variety of activities such as camping, hiking, knot-tying, target practice, and creative projects. These activities are designed to help our Scouts develop their skills, exercise their resourcefulness, increase their self-reliance, exemplify teamwork, share responsibility, experience a sense of belonging, nurture their self-esteem, feel successful, and learn life lessons. The Scouting program is an effective way to prepare young people to make ethical and moral choices over their lifetimes by instilling in them the values of the Scout Oath and Law.

Join Now!

  1. Contact one of our Pack Leaders to let us know that you would like to join.
  2. Attend a Pack Meeting. We generally meet on the 3rd Thursday of each month. Contact one of our Pack Leaders to confirm the next meeting. After registering, your youth will meet their assigned Den, which is a small group of same-aged children.
  3. You're in! Start attending Den Meetings and Pack Meetings and let the fun begin!

Pack Information

  • How often do you meet?
    We conduct a Pack meeting or Pack activity once a month, where all of us get together. Additionally, we hold Den Meetings once or twice per month, where the same-aged children get together at the Den Leader’s house, local park, or other designated location. The Den Meetings are where much of the learning happens, so you’ll want to attend as many of those as you can.
  • Where does the Pack meet?
    Pack Meetings: Twin Creeks Park Pavillion.
    Den Meetings: At the home of your Den Leader or another mutually agreed upon location.
  • When are Pack Meetings?
    3rd Thursday of each month from 6:30 PM - 8:00 PM.
  • When are Den Meetings?
    The Den Leader decides with the agreement of the families in the Den.
  • What is a Den?
    A Den, similar to a school classroom, consists of children of the same age. It's typically led by a parent, known as the Den Leader, who is a registered leader in the Boy Scouts of America. The Den Leader can appoint a Scout, or Denner, as a Youth Leader. Additionally, a Scout from a nearby troop, known as a Den Chief, may also serve as a Youth Leader if assigned. However, the adult Den Leader always maintains authority.
  • Who can join?
    All K-5 children, regardless of gender or school, are welcome. Most of our Scouts come from Deer Creek, Westside, and Faubion Elementary Schools. We also have Scouts from Naumann, Sterling Classical School, other nearby schools, and the local homeschool community.
  • To which Boy Scout Troops do the Cubs Cross Over?
    Cub Scouts are free to cross-over to any Boy Scout Troop they choose. However, our Cub Scouts tend to cross over to Troop 2008 or Troop 271.
  • How can I volunteer to help out?
    All parents are welcome and encouraged to volunteer to help the Pack. We need volunteers in all positions. Please attend a meeting and speak with a Pack Leader to learn about open positions.

Do Amazing Things!

Cub Scouts offers your child and family a unique opportunity to participate in activities that are not available anywhere else. These activities include:

  • Camping in the outdoors
  • Building model rockets
  • Sleeping overnight inside NASA Houston
  • Participating in the Pinewood Derby
  • Sleeping overnight in a zoo
  • Visiting fire stations and police stations
  • Meeting the Mayor and City Council
  • Participate in parades
  • ...and more!

These activities are designed to be fun and engaging while also teaching your child important life skills such as leadership, teamwork, and problem-solving. Cub Scouts is a great way to help your child develop into a well-rounded individual and prepare them for the future.
