Cub Scout Pack 1720

of Cedar Park, Texas

Open to Boy and Girls, Grades K-5

Cub Scouts Pack 1720, is a Cub Scout Pack located in Cedar Park, a suburb Northwest of Austin Texas. We are part of the North Short District of the Capitol Area Council . Our Charter Organization is Peace Lutheran Church.

The Scouting program has specific objectives, commonly referred to as the "Aims of Scouting." They are character formation, citizenship training, and personal fitness. Leadership development is also one of Scoutings eight methods contributing to both good character and good citizenship. The Boy Scouts achieves the Aims of Scouting via the Scouting Program.

The Scouting Program contains age-appropriate challenges delivered in a safe environment, where kids can achieve and fail and let the experience be their teacher. The activies are designed to be fun so that this growth experience isn't like school or the military. Many of the activities can involve the whole family, whatever that looks like. Scouting is about raising the next generation of adults. It is the kind of enrichment that builds the whole person into the kind of adults that we all want them to become.

Information

  • Where does the pack meet?
    Milburn Park Pavillion
  • When are Pack Meetings?
    3rd Thursday of the Month from 6:30pm-8:00pm
  • Who can join?
    Any child, boy or girl, K-5 regardless of school. That said, most Scouts hail from Deer Creek Elementary. A close second is Westside Elementary. We also have Scouts who attend Naumann, Sterling Classical School and other nearby schools. Additionally, we have a share of Homeschooled children from the area as well. All children are welcome.
  • Which Boy Scout Troops do the Cubs Cross Over to?
    Cub Scouts may cross-over to any troop they choose. There is no set troop. That said, the Troops that our cubs tend to cross over to are: Troop 2008 , Troop 271
  • How can I volunteer to help out?
    Any parent is welcome (and encouraged!) to volunteer to help the Pack. We need volunteer parents in all positions. Please come to a meeting and speak with a Pack Leader to hear about open positions.
  • I am an Eagle Scout parent
    We love Eagle Scout parents. Please come to a meeting and speak with a Pack Leader to hear about open positions.

COVID-19 Policy

Cub Scouts Pack 1720 will continue to abide by all rules, policies and guidelines set down by State and Local Governments, as well as our Charter Organization (Peace Lutheran Church) as well as any rules, policies and guidelines handed down by the Boy Scouts of America, Capitol Area Council, North Shore District or any park, school, or venue that we use.

For the foreseeable future, we intend to hold all our activities outdoors and ask the children to socially distance as well as to wear masks while indoors. Since all of our Scouts are too young for the vaccine, we recognize that every single one of them is vulnerable to COVID.

Fun Fact: Our current Cubmaster is an actual infectious disease epidemeologist with 20 years experience. We are blessed to have his knowledge and experience as part of our leadership in the Pack.
